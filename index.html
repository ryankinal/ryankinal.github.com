6/2/2016 - Ramping Up

Last weekend, I was sitting on the shore of Lake Erie watching the sunset. I sat there for probably an hour, watching the sun peek in and out of clouds on its way toward the horizon. And I had an epiphany. I needed to get working on music. Seriously.

I know a ton of musicians who are making a go of it, gigging around town, around the region, and around the country. Why couldn't that be me? And, given my technical background, why couldn't I also incorporate that? I have the equipment for recording, and, especially, streaming. There's no need to limit shows to when I can travel, when the intarwub makes it possible to perform live any time I want to, and for anybody who will listen.

So, I started preparing. I've made a list. I've made phone calls. And here's the plan:

Step 1: Practice Space

My third-floor apartment isn't exactly ideal for this plan. I can't amplify, and I even feel bad singing too loudly. While I'm in my apartment, I try not to play music past 9:00 P.M., and due to the work hours of some other tenants, I only play quietly during the day.

I need a new space

Step 2: Streaming

I have a Twitch account. I have the software. I have good mics, and a good webcam. I just need to get Step 1 into place in order to start using them to stream.

I want to stream at least two nights per week, one of which will be more of a performance (probably Tuesdays), and one of which will be more practice-oriented (probably Thursdays). I doubt I'd stream the writing process - it's boring and repetetive, and highly personal. But maybe streaming recording sessions on the weekends would be cool too.

Step 3: Local Marketing

I have a small following locally. There are at least some people that know my name. I just need to push the gigs. I need to get my business cards out there (now that I have them), and create more of a following. Play live. Have fun. "Press the Flesh" as they say

So there's my plan. There are a couple more steps, like branding and merchandise, and Patreon. But these are a few good steps to get me started.

Looking forward to it.
